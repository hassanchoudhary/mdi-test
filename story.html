OUR STORY

The majestic structure of Mas des Infermières, along the road of Saint Sebastien, is in the plain of Les Poulivets, a settlement just outside of Oppède-Le-Vieux.

The silence and sunshine are a sanctuary from my profession. I love to come here and paint, read, write and, hopefully, improve my area of Provence.

After ten years in the English Cotswold countryside, which was very beautiful but also quite damp, I was encouraged to look for a sunny place in Provence.

I found the climate and light of Provence very relaxing and charming; it explained the reason for the area being so prolific and attractive to the great school of painters that lived here in the first half of the 20th century — Picasso and Van Gogh no less, and others.

I bought a pretty “Manoir” in 1992, with 11 hectares of newly planted vines with American rootstock, just one kilometer from Oppède-le-Vieux. For ten years, my crop was harvested by the co-operative at Coustellet. In this time, I was lucky to meet Denis Langue, an expert winemaker. I was curious to see what my vines were producing after 17 years of maturing in the Oppède soil and sun.

EXPLORE

After ten years in the English Cotswold countryside, which was very beautiful but also quite damp, I was encouraged to look for a sunny place in Provence.

I found the climate and light of Provence very relaxing and charming; it explained the reason for the area being so prolific and attractive to the great school of painters that lived here in the first half of the 20th century — Picasso and Van Gogh no less, and others.

I bought a pretty “Manoir” in 1992, with 11 hectares of newly planted vines with American rootstock, just one kilometer from Oppède-le-Vieux. For ten years, my crop was harvested by the co-operative at Coustellet. In this time, I was lucky to meet Denis Langue, an expert winemaker. I was curious to see what my vines were producing after 17 years of maturing in the Oppède soil and sun.

Denis’ comment and verdict were; “Promising grapes, you should consider producing your own label.” Soon thereafter we arranged to make the wine with Cave de Lumières, only 3 kilometers from the house, and I became a bona fide vintner.

By 2012 we were awarded a Gold Medal at the Paris “Concours General Agricole,” a Silver medal in 2015, and again, another Gold in 2016.

Clearly, it was time to consider building a Cave in the Oppède vineyard. In 2017 it was designed with the help of a local architect, Andrew Corpe from Bonnieux — an Englishman who has been working in Provence for over 30 years. We broke ground in late 2018, it will be completed by late 2020.

In the 25 years I have been visiting this magical area, it has become an important place for me with its rich history — In 2009 I made a film there celebrating my love for the district and the French way of life, “A Good Year”. The silence and sunshine are a sanctuary from my profession — filming is noisy, and stress filled, but it is my passion, and I live it. I love to come here and paint, read, write and, hopefully, improve my corner of Provence.

I hope you enjoy our wine!

MEET THE TEAM

Fabian Lacassaigne

Grandson of a winegrower in the Bordeaux region, I spent my youth near Sauternes, where the passion for wine appeared to me. I’ve started studies in viticulture and oenology which enabled me to obtain my first job as a cellar master in the Entre-deux-Mers appellation. This experience allowed me to start working in the prestigious Pessac-Leognan appellation at Château Luchey-Halde.

Thomas Camilleri

Thomas is a sommelier graduated from the university of wine of Suze-la-Rousse. He is a real wine and gastronomy enthusiast especially regarding the product of his native region, the southern rhône valley. He has been working in a french gourmet market in Los Angeles and was the wine tourism manager of another winery on the appellation Luberon.

Melanie de Rudder

Born in Avignon, I grew up in the capital of the Côtes du Rhône, amazed by the varied landscapes and valleys of the surrounding vineyards. After studies in Biology (where I discovered the protection of the vine) and a business school, I immediately went into a profession of passion. 17 years as sales manager in important wine estates have allowed me to develop my knowledge of wines and in particular the various appellations and crus of the southern Rhône Valley which I especially love. I then took a big step forward, by creating my own company ... to select and support various talented winegrowers in their business development and to offer buyers unique and quality wines.

OUR STORY

ENVIRONMENT

At the core of our environmental strategy and policy is the protection and the development of the bee population on the Mas. Indeed, as the old proverb says, “if the bees die, we all die”. This fundamental goal has implications on all aspects of our viticulture, starting with a state-of-the-art geographic information system to optimize all treatments in the vineyards, reducing our carbon footprint and minimizing the use of any chemicals, whether organic or not. In order to become self-sufficient waterwise, we are conducting a

hydrological study using Ground Penetrating Radar data, to optimize the choice of our drilling sites as well as to map the sustainable reserves of underground water. The bee protection priority has led us to pursue the environmentally friendly certification, HVE, Haute Valeur Environmentale (which translates to High Environmental Value) rather than organic certification. This allows the use of only one insecticide - Pyrethrin, a known bee killer.
